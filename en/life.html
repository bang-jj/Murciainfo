Life Guide: Food & Supermarkets

How to survive in Murcia: From budget dining to grocery shopping tips.

1. Eating Out

While restaurants in Spain easily cost 15-20€, exchange students usually rely on fast food, Kebabs, or microwave meals from supermarkets.

🍔 Fast Food (Average cost: 8-10€)

  • Plaza Circular Area: Popeyes, KFC, and Burger King are available. Note: The BK near El Corte Inglés can be crowded with teenagers and offers average service.
  • Senda de Granada: The BK next to Carrefour offers a more comfortable environment than the city center.
  • McDonald's: Located towards the shopping mall (Churra stop). Highly recommended to download the App for coupons, but note that there are no free refills here.
  • Others: IKEA (Príncipe Felipe stop) and Taco Bell are also affordable options.

🌯 Kebab (Top pick for under 10€)

Found almost everywhere. Recommended spots: Hakan, Musa, Mundo Kebab. Combo meals usually include a main (Pita/Box), fries, and a drink—large portions and reliable choices.

🍕 Mid-range & Celebrations (Average cost: 12-20€+)

  • Pizza/Pasta: La Pierotti Pizzeria, Mano a mano.
  • Group Dining: Anari Sushi (Buffet), MISO (Japanese Buffet), DaLongYi Hotpot.

2. Grocery Shopping & Cooking

⚠️ Important Warning:
Except for Carrefour, local supermarkets (Mercadona, Lidl, etc.) are **CLOSED on Sundays and public holidays**! Make sure to stock up in advance.

🛒 Local Supermarkets

Mercadona, Día, Consum, Lidl, El Corte Inglés. You can easily find basic necessities like oil, salt, rice, potatoes, and cabbage—very similar to what you'd find back home.

🏮 Asian Supermarkets

If you miss Asian greens, daikon radish, black vinegar, or Shacha sauce, head to these spots:

  • Hello Asia: Located in the city center, a 10-minute walk from Plaza Circular.
  • Happy Dulce (糖家福): Located along the Bus 44 route; relatively easy to access.
  • Wan Ka Le (萬家樂): Located in the suburbs; requires Bus 30.

3. Mobile & Internet

Upon arriving in Spain, it is recommended to visit a mobile store to get a Prepaid Card (Prepago). No long-term contracts are required.

📱 Major Providers & Application

  • Recommended Companies: Orange, Vodafone, and Movistar (Not only these three).
  • Required Document: You must bring your Original Passport to apply for a physical SIM card.
  • Estimated Cost: A monthly plan of 10€ to 15€ is usually more than enough for data and calls.

💸 Renewal & Topping Up (Validity: 28 Days)

Prepaid plans in Spain typically run on a 28-day cycle. There are two ways to renew:

  • Online Top-up: Your provider will send an SMS reminder before your plan expires with a link. Click the link to pay and renew online.
  • In-person (Cash Only): If you cannot pay online, visit any Tabacos (Tobacco shop). Provide the clerk with your phone number and the amount to top up. Note: This method is usually cash only.
