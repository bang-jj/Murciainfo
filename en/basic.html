📍 About Murcia: City & Campus

Murcia: City Overview

Murcia is the seventh-largest city in Spain, known for its warm and dry climate. The cost of living is significantly lower compared to Madrid or Barcelona. However, entertainment options are somewhat limited; most extracurricular activities center around the city center (Plaza Circular) or the large shopping mall (Nueva Condomina). Other residential areas include Guadalupe, La Ñora, Senda de Granada, and Espinardo. While the city center offers excellent convenience, living in other areas may require purchasing daily necessities in bulk. Many locals outside the academic environment do not speak English, so learning basic Spanish for communication is highly recommended.

UCAM University Environment

UCAM (Universidad Católica San Antonio de Murcia) is renowned for its excellence in sports. The main campus is located at the Los Jerónimos Monastery, accessible by bus or tram, approximately 30-60 minutes from the city center. Programs taught in English include Psychology, Business, Modern Languages, Dentistry, Pharmacy, Sports Science, and International Relations.
